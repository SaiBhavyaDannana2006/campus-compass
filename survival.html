Establish side-by-side academic and campus lifestyle boundaries to keep your academic progress optimal and bypass infractions.

The Do's & The Don'ts of Amrita

These are not standard guidelines. These are absolute golden boundaries formulated from real hostel room debriefs.

The Absolute Do's

Check Student Mail Daily (@am.students.amrita.edu)

Official channels utilize standard Outlook emails. Updates on exam sheets, fee due dates, registrations, and club logs land here. Missing a mail could void evaluations.

Secure 100% of Continuous lab Marks CGPA Booster

Laboratories award 30 marks directly via observation sheets, execution records, and signed code outputs. Clean, complete submissions guarantee a strong CGPA buffer.

Respect Biometrics & Curfew Protocols

Residents must swipe thumbs at biometric scanners every evening post-dinner. Missed biometric checks trigger automated notification alerts sent directly to registered parent contacts.

Network with Active Seniors

Seniors manage critical textbook directories, past year papers, code templates, and project codebases. Cultivate constructive relationships to access peer-to-peer repositories.

The Absolute Don'ts

Don't Skirt Close to 75.0% Attendance Limit DANGER ZONE

Maintain a safety margin well above 75.0%. Slipping even slightly below (e.g., 74.8%) due to late illness triggers an automatic, unresolvable 'FA' (Attendance Failure).

Don't Copy-Paste Lab codes Block-by-Block

System lab platforms utilize the MOSS plagiarism analyzer. Copying code scripts triggers flag alerts, leading to immediate zero credits or referral to institutional committees.

Don't Miss the 8:30 PM Curfew Threshold

Curfew lockout begins at 8:30 PM. Late arrivals are reported to the institutional discipline heads and charged a 2,000 regulatory penalty fine.

Don't Trap Yourself in Silent Comparison Loops

Avoid comparing progress with advanced peers. Everyone begins at individual baseline layers. Work consistently on your own repository goals at your distinct pace.

Managing Your Fresh Freedom (A Senior's Brotherly Advice)

For many, college is the first taste of unsupervised freedom. Late-night sessions, skipped meals, and study procrastination are temptingly easy traps.

The 11:00 PM Rule: Guarding Your Sleep Cycles

Missing 9:00 AM lectures leads directly to low grades and attendance fails. Establish an absolute 11:00 PM screen-off ritual to secure 7+ structural hours of deep rest.

Dealing with Academic Comparison & Peer Pressure

Tech classes contain students from diverse backgrounds, some with early programming experience. Avoid toxic comparisons.

"Your only competition is the person in the mirror. Write 10 lines of clean, logical code today that you couldn't write yesterday, and you are already winning."

Build a reliable peer circle of 3-4 classmates who work on labs honestly. Keep your room clean, eat well, and call your family daily.
