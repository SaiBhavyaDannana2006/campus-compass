Technical Hubs vs. Cultural & Sports Associations

Join exactly one Technical club for development skills, and one Cultural/Sports club for balance and networking.

Technical Hubs (Development & Hacking)

Amrita Tech Events Signature Hub

Chakravyaha (The Tech Events Guild)

The premier tech events and competitive coding guild. They design and host Amrita’s signature engineering tournaments, capture-the-flag (CTF) security contests, and algorithmic challenges to crack recruitment barriers.

Why It Matters:

Builds leadership, coordinates mega university fests, and develops rigorous algorithmic problem-solving stamina.

Hackathons & Dev Sprints Intense Builders

Prachurya (The Hack Club & Dev Space)

A high-octane builders' playground for building full-stack applications, mobile apps, and systems engineering. They organize overnight hackathons, system design bootcamps, and open-source contribution sprints.

Why It Matters:

Drives full-stack portfolio building with continuous 30-hour multi-track code sprints and real deployment pipelines.

AI & Deep Learning Intelligent Systems

ReLu (The Artificial Intelligence Club)

Named after the Rectified Linear Unit activation function, ReLu is Amrita Amaravati's premier neural networks and AI research group. They build active projects spanning Large Language Models (LLMs), deep learning, computer vision, and tensor pipeline optimization.

Why It Matters:

Demystifies state-of-the-art machine intelligence. Hands-on model engineering, weights training, and API proxy development.

Cultural & Creative Associations (Sanity & Bond)

Music & Vocal Resonance Melodic Heartbeat

Saptaswara (Acoustic & Fusion Music Club)

The official musical heartbeat of the campus. Saptaswara nurtures classical ragas, acoustic bands, and western fusion, conducting weekly open-air garden jam sessions and backing all primary cultural events.

Why It Matters:

Critical acoustic workspace to unlock rhythmic talents, de-stress, and form close collaborative friendships.

Video & Photography Cinematic Eye

Drisya (Filmmaking & Photography Club)

The modern content engine and visual gallery creator of Amrita Amaravati. Drisya is a high-impact filmmakers' and photographers' alliance hosting 28-day film challenges, screenplay writing bootcamps, and digital design masterclasses.

Why It Matters:

Prepares you for screen directing, editing, videography, and advanced photographic setups. Integrates creative technology with visual aesthetics.

Dance & Choreography Rhythmic Motion

Nithyasparsh (The Dance Club)

The energetic expressions and choreography federation of Amrita. Nithyasparsh celebrates classical Bharatanatyam, contemporary hip-hop, and energetic fusion performances that light up regional and inter-college cultural festivals.

Why It Matters:

Inspires spatial design, synchronicity, stage confidence, and physical fitness through fluid rhythmic performance.

The Process Defined

How & When to Join the Guilds

Recruitment is formal, standardized, and competitive. Follow this exact timeline to secure your induction.

Week 1 — 2
Week 1 — 2

Orientation & Freshers Stalls

Meet student clubs at corridors stalls during official freshman events. Chat with senior members, ask about commitments, and collect pamphlets.

Action: Zero eligibility filters. Just explore!
Week 3
Week 3

Official Email Loops & Application Forms

Monitor your @am.students.amrita.edu inbox. Submit standardized application forms sent by the Student Affairs Council.

Critical Step: Make sure your form answers are genuine.
Week 4
Week 4

Selection Interviews & Hack Rounds

Attend physical evaluations in assigned classrooms. Tech clubs conduct coding tasks or review project portfolios. Cultural clubs host active performance auditions.

Tip: Seniors search for passion and consistency, not expert status.
Week 5
Week 5

Final Result Release & Onboarding

Induction lists are published via official emails or Microsoft Teams rooms. Inductions start immediately with introductory tasks and senior meetups.
